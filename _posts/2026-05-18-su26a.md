title: Mesh-Prompted Anatomy Segmentation
abstract: We present a novel technique for segmenting anatomical structures in medical
  images by using a canonical mesh as a prompt for the structure to be segmented.
  Unlike point-prompted segmentation methods, such as those based on Segment-Anything
  Models, mesh prompting reduces the ambiguity associated with point prompts and provides
  a stronger shape prior, which is particularly advantageous for many medical applications.
  Our approach performs mesh-prompted segmentation by registering the signed distance
  function (SDF) of the mesh to the target image using a vector-field attention network
  trained with boundary-based loss terms. Before registration, the prompted mesh is
  roughly aligned with the structure in the target image using a center prompt provided
  by the user. This method allows for independent initialization of each structureś
  position and the prediction of deformation fields specific to each structure, which
  offers advantages over segmentation via direct image registration that typically
  relies on a single deformation field to accommodate all structures. Additionally,
  it preserves surface correspondence better than image registration using region-based
  loss terms. We evaluate our method on two CT datasets featuring common ear and body
  structures. A comparison of our technique with image registration and other state-of-the-art
  segmentation methods shows that our approach achieves superior segmentation accuracy.
