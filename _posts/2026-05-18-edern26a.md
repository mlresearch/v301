title: 'FLAIRBrainSeg: Fine-Grained Brain Segmentation Using FLAIR MRI Only'
abstract: This paper introduces a novel method for brain segmentation using only FLAIR
  MRIs, specifically targeting cases where access to other imaging modalities is limited.
  By leveraging existing automatic segmentation methods, we train a network to approximate
  segmentations, typically obtained from T1-weighted MRIs. Our method, called FLAIRBrainSeg,
  produces segmentations of 132 structures and is robust to multiple sclerosis lesions.
  Experiments on both in-domain and out-of-domain datasets demonstrate that our method
  outperforms modality-agnostic approaches based on image synthesis, the only currently
  available alternative for performing brain parcellation using FLAIR MRI alone. This
  technique holds promise for scenarios where T1-weighted MRIs are unavailable and
  offers a valuable alternative for clinicians and researchers in need of reliable
  anatomical segmentation.
